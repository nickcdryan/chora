Chora Text Editor

What is this?

Chora is a writing style inspired by music, in particular fugues. Just as a fugue weaves multiple melodic voices together to create a complex, harmonious composition, Chora uses multiple voices simultaneously to create a complex, harmonious piece of fiction.

Conventional writing incorrectly borrows its format from the tradition of oral storytelling. A speaker is constrained to speak one word after another in linear order - conventional writing borrows this format putting one word after another in linear order. However, reading and writing are a visual medium. A writer is not compelled to read out his text orally, word by word. A reader can pause, go back, skip forward, examine an illustration, or understand multiple pieces as working together simultaneously, in the same way that an audience can read or listen to music involving multiple voices or instruments played simultaneously.

Reading should therefore be recognized as a visual medium. A reader is not constrained to read one word at a time in linear order any more than he is constrained to examine a painting pixel by pixel, or to hear music one instrument at a time. Chora is fiction for the visual medium.

How it works

Write across multiple "voices" of text that flow in parallel. Each voice can represent perspective, dialogue, narrative, sensation, or any other element of your writing.
